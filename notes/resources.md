# Resources

## GOES, MODIS, and VIIRS

Website:
https://www.nesdis.noaa.gov/news/wildfires-space-how-the-view-above-helps-firefighters-the-ground

Purpose:
How satellites assist in wildfire monitoring in polar orbits and GEO

Useful Information:
- GOES remains in GEO and is used to see first embers or wildfires.
- GOES provides continuous, near-real time monitoring
- MODIS informed design of VIIRS
- VIIRS provides smaller, lower-temp fire detections day and night

## How NOAA monitors wildfires

Website:
https://www.nesdis.noaa.gov/our-environment/dust-ash-fire/fire

Purpose:
How GOES satellites and VIIRS are used to monitor wildfires

Useful Information:
- GOES provides continuous, near-real time monitoring in geostationary orbit
- GOES helps forecasters detect and characterize fires
- VIIRS provides smaller, lower-temp fire detections day and night, used in LEO orbits

## Comparing terrestrial, UAV, and satellite methods of wildfire monitoring

Website:
https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7697165/

Purpose:
Compares geostationary satellites, SSO satellites, and Cubesats

Useful Information:
- GEO provides high temporal resolution but low spatial resolution
- SSO provide low temporal resolution but high spatial resolution
- Cubesats provide good temporal and spatial resolution when in satellite constellations
